## Persona C — The Established SMB / Service Provider
**Role in strategy:** Revenue anchor. NOW market.

**Who they are:** Clinics, gyms, beauty salons, legal offices, financial advisors, real estate agencies. Established customer base, formalizing digital communication channels.

**Jobs to be done:**
* Provide consistent, professional customer service across WhatsApp, email, and social — without hiring a dedicated support team.
* Utilize a unified knowledge base **(new)** to quickly answer repetitive client questions.
* Standardize how the team handles digital communication (moving away from personal WhatsApp accounts).

**Pains:**
* A clinic misses a patient follow-up because it was buried in a personal WhatsApp thread.
* Customer service quality is inconsistent because there's no system — it depends on who's working that day.

**Aspirations & Emotional Layer (new):** They want the operational maturity of a massive corporation. They want to eliminate the anxiety of "Did we reply to that client?" and replace it with the confidence of a perfectly centralized, professional operation.

**Primary Tier:** Growth Tier (2-3 seats) or Scale Tier depending on team size.

**Value proposition for them:** Everything you need for seamless customer support, all in one place. Live chat with your customers anywhere, manage bookings, and maintain a professional social presence without needing a massive team.

## Persona D — The E-Commerce Brand
**Role in strategy:** High volume. NOW market.

**Who they are:** DTC brands on Shopify, WooCommerce, Mercado Libre. Often founder-led at early stage, but transitioning to team-led.

**Jobs to be done:**
* Handle post-purchase support (tracking, returns, order issues) at volume without degrading quality.
* Transition from "founder answers WhatsApp personally" to "team handles support professionally."
* Maintain community and campaign content presence while support volume scales.

**Pains:**
* Managing WhatsApp through personal phones breaks down exactly when the brand starts to succeed — at the worst possible moment.
* Content scheduling and customer support are treated as separate problems requiring separate tools, causing data silos.

**Aspirations & Emotional Layer (new):** They want to break through their current growth ceiling. They aspire to build a recognizable, beloved brand identity where the marketing promises match the post-purchase support experience seamlessly.

**Primary Tier:** Growth Tier transitioning to Scale Tier.

**Value proposition for them:** The omnichannel infrastructure that grows with you. Handle WhatsApp, email, and social support from one team inbox — before your current system breaks under the weight of your success. And run your content campaigns from the exact same place.
